# vein Pitch Script

> **Duration:** 5 minutes (10 slides). Appendix slides are for Q&A only.
> **Pace:** ~30 seconds per slide. Breathe between slides.
> **Rule:** Say less than what's on the slide. The audience reads — you add context and emotion.

---

## Slide 1: Title
*[Dark slide, logo animates in]*

> "Hi, I'm [Name] from vein.
>
> vein is the operating system for healthcare staffing in Thailand.
>
> Today, when a clinic needs a nurse for tonight's shift, they post in a LINE group and hope someone replies. We replace that with verified workforce infrastructure — filling shifts in hours, not days."

**~20 seconds. Don't over-explain. Move.**

## Slide 2: Problem
*[Light slide, three stat cards animate in]*

> "Healthcare staffing in Thailand is broken — and we didn't just assume that, we measured it.
>
> We analyzed 289,000 lines of real LINE group chat across 5 active staffing groups. Here's what we found:
>
> Thailand is short over 51,000 nurses in the public sector alone. Up to 57% of new nursing graduates quit within their first year. And the agencies filling this gap? They charge a 30 to 40 percent markup on every single shift.
>
> The entire market — nurses, clinics, hospitals, nursing homes — runs on LINE group chats and phone calls. No platform. No verification. No accountability. When a nurse doesn't show up, which happens constantly, there's no recourse. The facility just posts again tomorrow."

**~45 seconds. This is the emotional hook. Let the numbers land. Pause after "no recourse."**

---

## Slide 3: Solution
*[Dark slide, pipeline steps animate left to right]*

> "vein handles the full staffing lifecycle in one platform.
>
> Verify — we check credentials before a nurse can book a shift. Match — facilities post structured shifts with transparent pay, nurses browse and apply. Confirm — we track attendance and completion. Pay — reliable payout with tax documentation. Trust — two-way reviews build portable reputation.
>
> *[Point to screenshot]* This is the actual product. Not a mockup — a working app. Provider login through LINE, shift browsing, booking, credential upload, reviews. The facility side has shift creation, applicant management, and attendance confirmation. 59 routes, built and deployed."

**~40 seconds. Speed through the pipeline steps. Slow down on "this is the actual product."**

## Slide 4: Why Now
*[Light slide, three cards + LINE callout]*

> "Three things are converging right now.
>
> First — Thailand just became a complete aged society. 13.6 million elderly, outnumbering children. The country will be super-aged by the early 2030s — faster than the US, faster than the UK. 900,000 new seniors every year, each one potentially needing care.
>
> Second — the nurse shortage is structural. 7,000 nurses leave state hospitals every year. The government announced a 10-year plan to recruit 124,000 more — that tells you how deep the gap is.
>
> Third — zero digital tools exist. We checked. 329 Thai healthtech startups, zero do workforce staffing.
>
> And here's the unlock: 56 million Thais are on LINE. 86% of internet users. Every nurse we observed already uses LINE for work. We don't need to change behavior — we upgrade the tool they already use."

**~50 seconds. The LINE callout is the punchline. Build to it.**

## Slide 5: Market
*[Light slide, concentric circles: TAM → SAM → SOM]*

> "The Southeast Asia healthcare staffing market is 20.7 billion baht, growing at 10% per year. That's our TAM.
>
> Thailand has never been formally sized — because the market is almost entirely informal. So we sized it ourselves using three independent methods: supply-side, demand-side, and top-down. All three converge between 3.3 and 3.9 billion baht. That's a 17% spread — within the validation threshold.
>
> Our Year 1 target is conservative: 1.8 to 3.6 million baht in platform revenue. That's 0.1% of the Bangkok market. US platforms captured 2 to 5 percent in their first year."

**~35 seconds. Don't get into methodology here — that's what Appendix A1 is for. Just state the numbers confidently.**

## Delivery Notes

- **Don't read the slides.** The audience reads faster than you talk. Add context the slides don't show.
- **Pause after big numbers.** Let 51,400 and 289,000 land. Silence after a number is more powerful than rushing to the next point.
- **Make eye contact during "we lived it"** (Slide 9) and "the market is open" (Slide 8). These are the two emotional peaks.
- **If someone interrupts with a question** — answer briefly, say "I have more detail in the appendix," and continue. Don't let Q&A hijack the flow.
- **Appendix slides are your safety net.** If asked about market sizing methodology → A1. Competitive detail → A2. Roadmap → A3. Unit economics → A4. GTM channels → A5.
